Car Game Project 2D is one of my most recent projects, developed entirely from scratch using C++ and OpenGL.

While it may appear simple at first—with PNG-based graphics and limited player interaction—it required significant effort due to the low-level nature of both C++ and OpenGL, which lack the conveniences of higher-level languages, APIs, or modern game frameworks.

This project builds upon the foundations laid in earlier efforts, including the Traffic Simulator 2D, also developed using C++ and OpenGL.

Game Mechanics

The objective is to drive through traffic while avoiding collisions. The player earns:
  • +10 points for overtaking another car,
  • –5 points for colliding with a traffic car,
  • –1 point for each car that overtakes the player.
  • Game Over occurs when the player's car accumulates over 100% damage.
Additional gameplay dynamics:
  • The player's speed is significantly reduced when leaving the road or during collisions.
  • Damage severity depends on the type and direction of the collision:
  • Side collisions cause minor damage.
  • Frontal collisions with cars moving in the same direction cause moderate damage.
  • Frontal collisions with cars moving in the opposite direction cause severe damage due to the combined impact forces.

Technical Challenges and Solutions

✓ Road and Curves
OpenGL lacks built-in support for curved surfaces, so I had to experiment with mathematical functions and rendering techniques (e.g., scanline drawing, quad distortion) to simulate a dynamically curving road.

✓ Traffic Car AI
To ensure AI cars follow the road correctly, I implemented a custom system that adjusts their position and heading based on the road's curvature. This allows AI cars to steer naturally, regardless of direction.

✓ Animation and Effects
Without built-in animation support, I developed custom logic for effects. For example, on collision, the area around the player's car fades in and out by modifying opacity over time.

✓ Texture-Based HUD
OpenGL doesn't support text rendering natively. I used SDL_Surface objects to render text and converted them into OpenGL textures to build a heads-up display (HUD).

✓ Traffic Car Spawning and Recycling
I implemented logic to spawn traffic cars while avoiding immediate collisions. A major challenge was managing cars that became stationary due to collisions—they would linger off-screen and block new spawns. I solved this by tracking Y-positions and recycling cars only when they leave the visible or active world space.

✓ Car Steering Animation
To simulate the car turning, I created a system where the player's car quad rotates proportionally to steering input, mimicking the behavior of more advanced game engines.

✓ Infinite World System
I built a looping world using reusable segments. As the player progresses, old segments are recycled to the end of the world queue, creating a seamless scrolling environment.
